Metacentrism Orientation Test

Question 1
You are finally home and relaxing after a difficult day.
A friend calls and asks you to come over immediately. They sound distressed but can’t explain why.




Question 2
You work in an electronics store.
Your manager instructs you to actively push a new extended insurance policy to customers, even though it’s unnecessary for them.




Question 3
You have a stable, low-stress job that pays enough.
A former high-school friend invites you to join their startup producing a new fishing rod.
You love fishing, the idea excites you, but you’d need to quit your job and accept lower pay and uncertainty for at least a year.




Question 4
You’re having dinner with friends.
Someone says your former school professor was boring and useless.
You personally liked that professor and found his classes meaningful.




Question 5
You’re on a business trip in a remote foreign country.
After you arrive, your boss calls and tells you that the project has been cancelled by the client.
Because of the location, the next flight home is only available in a week.
Your hotel and expenses are covered, and you have no work obligations.




Question 6
You’re on a bus.
Two older teenagers sitting across the aisle are calling a young schoolgirl, who is sitting in front of you, offensive names.
They are laughing. The girl is clearly distressed and crying.




Question 7
You work in a postal office where customer requests are accepted only through a website form.
An elderly man comes in and says a package he sent was never delivered.
He doesn’t understand the website and has no tracking number, but he has the receipt.
He asks you to help investigate.




Question 8
You attend a weekly photography class.
During a session, one participant asks the instructor whether phone cameras can be used for the final project.
The instructor laughs and says, “If you think phone photos belong here, maybe this class is too advanced for you.”
Several people smile. The participant looks down and stays quiet.




Question 9
You agreed a year ago to help a close relative run a small family café on weekends.
It’s not your main job, but they rely on you.
Recently, you’ve been feeling tired and less interested, and the café is doing fine enough without you.
No one forces you to stay, but leaving would clearly make things harder for them.




Question 10
You’re a freelancer.
New AI tools can now automate large parts of your work.
The results are cleaner but less customized.
Other freelancers are using the tools, finishing projects faster, and taking more work.
Clients don’t seem to notice the difference much.




Question 11
In your free time, you’ve been composing a rock opera.
You’ve been working on it seriously for a while and are proud of the result, but only a few close people know about it.
A friend suggests you start sharing parts of it online so more people can hear it.
